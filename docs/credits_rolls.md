# Credits Roll Behaviors (TGM Modes)

## TGM3 Easy
- Trigger: Reaching level 200 (after playfield clear animation).
- Duration: 55 seconds (per mode config).
- Play: Continuous piece spawns during roll; typically 20G gravity.
- Hanabi: Credits roll contributes hanabi using Easy-mode bonuses.
- Level: Clamped at 200 during roll.

## TGM3 Master
- Trigger: After reaching 999 (or configured completion) with grading active.
- Roll types: Disappearing (fading) or Invisible (m-roll) depending on performance.
- Grading: Staff-roll grading adds to final grade; COOL/REGRET/internal grade contribute before roll.
- Play: Roll is active gameplay; surviving affects final grade. Gravity high/20G per phase logic.

## TGM3 Shirase
- Trigger: After reaching level 1300 (or end condition) with torikan checks at 500/1000.
- Roll type: Big tetromino credit roll; monochrome blocks after 1000 before roll.
- Grading: Section-based S1–S13; roll completion indicated by line color (green reach, orange clear).
- Play: 20G with high speed; roll is active gameplay.

## TGM3 Sakura
- No traditional credits roll; mode ends after stages/EX completion. Time-attack results shown instead.

## General Notes
- Credits rolls are active gameplay (unless a specific mode disables input); topping out can affect outcome.
- Music: Credits BGM distinct from main BGM.
- Hanabi handling is mode-specific (Easy credits hanabi is counted; others generally not).

## TGM2
### Normal
- Trigger: Reaching the Normal endpoint (level 300 cap) after meeting mode conditions.
- Roll type: Disappearing roll (blocks fade after a short time).
- Grading: Surviving the roll influences clear status; no Ti-style staff-roll grading.
- Play: Active gameplay during roll; topping out ends the roll.
- Music: Credits BGM distinct from main BGM.

### T.A. Death
- Trigger: Reaching level 999 (with torikan checks) after meeting mode conditions.
- Roll type: Fast 20G disappearing roll aligned with the mode’s speed profile.
- Grading: Surviving the roll influences clear status; no Ti-style staff-roll grading.
- Play: Active gameplay during roll; topping out ends the roll.
- Music: Credits BGM distinct from main BGM.

### Master
- Trigger: Reaching the Master endpoint (level 999) with grading active.
- Roll type: Disappearing credit roll (blocks fade after a short delay), consistent with Master speed profile.
- Grading: Surviving the roll influences clear status; no Ti-style staff-roll grading, but completion affects final rank.
- Play: Active gameplay during roll; topping out ends the roll.
- Music: Credits BGM distinct from main BGM.
